### RITUCHARYA (SEASONAL) & YOGIC WISDOM

#### 1. GRISHMA RITU (Summer)
- **Climate**: Hot and dry; Pitta accumulates, Vata increases.
- **Diet**: Sweet, cold, liquid diet. Avoid spicy, sour, salty foods.
- **Yoga**: Cooling Pranayama (Sitali), gentle Moon Salutations. Avoid vigorous exercise.

#### 2. VARSHA RITU (Monsoon)
- **Climate**: Humid, rainy; Vata becomes aggravated, Pitta accumulates.
- **Diet**: Easy to digest, warm, oily. Boiled water. Small amount of honey.
- **Yoga**: Vata-balancing poses (Forward folds), alternate nostril breathing (Nadi Shodhana).

#### 3. SHARAD RITU (Autumn)
- **Climate**: Sudden heat after rains; Pitta becomes aggravated.
- **Diet**: Sweet, bitter, cooling. Ghee is highly recommended.
- **Yoga**: Twists to detox the liver, Sitali Pranayama.

#### 4. HEMANTA & SHISHIRA RITU (Winter)
- **Climate**: Cold and windy; Agni (digestive fire) is strongest.
- **Diet**: Heavy, nutritious, warm. Sesame oil is excellent.
- **Yoga**: Sun Salutations (Surya Namaskar), backbends, heating Pranayama (Bhastrika).

#### 5. VASANTA RITU (Spring)
- **Climate**: Warming, snow melts; Kapha becomes aggravated.
- **Diet**: Light, dry, bitter, pungent. Avoid cold/sweet foods.
- **Yoga**: Vigorous Sun Salutations, chest openers, Kapalabhati breathing.
